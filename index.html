For CS499 we were tasked with chooseing one to three artifacts that showcased our skills in three catigories: Software Design and Engineering, Algorithms and Data Structures, as well as databases. The artifact I chose is an inventory management application made in android studio. It allows the user to create, edit and maintain a list of items, with a strong focus on versatility in its design, meaning it is designed to be useful for multiple applications. This design is carried through throughout the app, in all functionality and elements. This shows my ability to design and develop a project with specific goals and requirements in mind.


Further enhacnments included sorting and search functionality as well as efficency improvents by eliminating and substituting costly lines of code like for loops. This portion of the project has shown me how far I have come as a programmer. Before the enhancements, there were too many for loops. For example, the app would do a linear search though the item’s array list multiple times in multiple places. In addition, the method to display the items to the recycler view, I had a loop that would do this just to create another list with the same information to be used later! I truly have no idea what I was thinking back then. Nearly all loops have been completely eliminated. Originally I planned on making the linear searches binary searches (this was in my outline), but as I worked on the enhancements I realized even those were not necessary with some adjustments. The app instead relies on passing information like indexes between activities or storing it in hidden ui elements instead of searching for the information every time it is needed.



This artifact shows my ability to create and maintain a database(s) for my applications and other solutions with a secure mindset. Furthermore, it underlines my ability to expose potential vulnerabilities, mitigate design flaws, and ensure privacy and enhanced security of data and resources.



Enhancing this artifact has taught me a lot. More specifically about working with an existing project, fixing its design flaws and enhancing it while also staying in line with the initial requirements of the project. There were a number of exceptions and odd behavior I had to track down. Moreover, I have learned how far I have come, fixing my old mistakes.
